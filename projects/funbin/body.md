after seeing a 2D histogram with absurdly out-of-place hexagonal binning,
I decided that this should be taken to the exteme. I wrote a simple script
to use arbitrary polygons as bins and started producing 2D histograms
binned in all sorts of crazy shapes. some example results are below

![example distribution binned in various shapes](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/nj-vs-vh/funbin/4b2bfc780f9e5ccd22a4a32a5aad4ac0d343bde4/media/demo.png)

this also lead to an April Fools paper, available on [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.30006),
that makes the case for using non-standard binnings, drawing from ancient mysticism, medieval
alchemy, and early modern philosopy.
